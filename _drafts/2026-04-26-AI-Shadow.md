## **Your Data, Their Models: How to Manage Privacy on Leading AI Platforms**
As AI tools become more integrated into our daily workflows, major providers like **Anthropic**, **OpenAI**, and **Google** are increasingly using user interactions to refine their models. While this can lead to improved performance, it also raises significant privacy concerns about the use and retention of personal data. In a growing digital world, how can this affect your personal and professional daily lives?

Here is a breakdown of how they handle your data and the specific steps you can take to maintain **your privacy**.

---

## **Anthropic (Claude)**
As of **August 28, 2025**, Anthropic updated its terms to allow data from **Claude Free, Pro, and Max** plans to be used for model training. This includes chats and coding sessions.
When opted-in for training, Anthropic extends its data retention period to **five years** to support long-term development. Otherwise, retention stays at the standard **30 days**.
These updates do **not** apply to business services like Claude for Work (Team/Enterprise) or API usage, which are excluded by default.
By the time of writing this article, Anthropic has already started training on data collected under the new terms, meaning that even if you opt-out now, your previous interactions may have already been used in training runs.

**How to Avoid Training:**
You can manage this at any time in your **Privacy Settings**. 

![Help Improve Claude toggle](/assets/img/posts/ai-shadow/claude-ai-settings.png)
*The "Help Improve Claude" toggle allows you to opt-out of training and keep your data retention at 30 days.*

## **OpenAI (ChatGPT)**
OpenAI may use content from individual services including ChatGPT, Codex, and Sora to improve model performance. This includes prompts, responses, images, and uploaded files.
By default, OpenAI does **not** train on data from **ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, or the API**. 

**How to Avoid Training (Web):**
1. Click your **profile icon** and select **Settings**.
2. Navigate to **Data Controls**.
3. Turn off **"Improve the model for everyone"**.

![ChatGPT Data Controls](/assets/img/posts/ai-shadow/open-ai-datacontrol-settings.png)
*The Data Controls menu in ChatGPT where you can disable model improvement.*

![Help Improve OpenAI toggle](/assets/img/posts/ai-shadow/open-ai-settings.png)
*The "Help Improve OpenAI" toggle allows you to opt-out of training.*

Even if you opt-out of training, if you provide explicit feedback in the chat (like a thumbs up or down), the **entire conversation** associated with that feedback may still be used for training.

OpenAI states that a limited number of authorized OpenAI personnel, as well as trusted service providers, may access user content. The main reasons are investigate abuse or a security incident, provide support to you if you reach out with questions about your account, handle legal matters and improve model performance (unless you have opted out). For these reasons, the company explicitly warns users not to enter sensitive information that they would not want reviewed or used.

To avoid all these risks, you can use the **Temporary Chat** mode, which ensures chats are not saved in your history, do not create memories, and are not used for training. These chats are deleted after 30 days and cannot be accessed by OpenAI for any purpose.

## **Google (Gemini)**
Gemini is deeply integrated with Google’s ecosystem, meaning it often has a larger data footprint involving your **Gmail, Calendar, and Drive** information.

**How to Avoid Training:**
1. Go to your **Gemini Apps Activity** page.
2. Under the "Gemini Apps Activity" section, click **Turn off**.
3. **Note:** Google warns that disabling this may prevent future conversations from being saved and could delete previous history.

![Google Privacy Settings](/assets/img/posts/ai-shadow/google-gemini-settings.png)
*Google privacy settings allow you to toggle off training.*

## **The Long-Term Risks of Data Sharing**

While these providers argue that data sharing creates a more capable and safer AI for everyone, this practice introduces significant **long-term risks** that every user should consider. 

The primary dangers associated with allowing our data to be used for model training include:

* **Permanent Data Imprints:** Once our information is used in a model training run, it becomes a permanent part of that model's "memory." Anthropic notes that if the user changes his mind later and opt out, all its data will **still be included in models that have already been trained**, meaning our "choice" to withdraw consent only applies to future training runs.
* **The Privacy vs. Utility Trade-off:** Companies often use **friction** to discourage privacy-seeking behavior. For example, Google warns users that disabling Gemini Apps Activity may result in the **loss of their chat history**, forcing users to choose between the convenience of a searchable history and the security of their data.

Ultimately, the "feedback loop" that helps AI get smarter is built on the **permanent exposure of our digital footprint**. For those handling truly sensitive information, relying on app-level toggles may not be enough, leading many to use **proactive guardrails** that stop data from ever leaving the browser.

### **Proactive Privacy Guardrails**
While app-specific settings are a start, they often come with "friction" designed to make you hesitate. 

* **General Toggles:** Most tools now include a master privacy toggle to anonymize or exclude your data.
* **Third-Party Tools:** Solutions like **Fendr Security** act as a proactive guardrail, stopping sensitive information from ever leaving your browser screen and reaching AI servers in the first place.
